ECE Instructional Lab Safety Requirements

Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE), UC Davis

Lab Safety Requirements

In all ECE instructional labs, the following safety rules must always be followed:

Collective Responsibility: Maintaining a safe lab environment is a collective responsibility. If a safety accident occurs during lab—whether to you or a fellow student, even if it is minor, immediately inform the teaching assistant, Lance Halsted (lehalsted@ucdavis.edu) or the instructor of the course.

Food and Drink: Food and drink are never allowed in the labs.

Electrical Safety: In the ECE electronic labs, only experiments and equipment with low voltages and currents are used. While the potential for electrical shock is low, follow these guidelines:

  • Always turn off power before making circuit modifications
  • Never touch exposed conductors in a powered circuit
  • Keep your work area dry and clean to prevent short circuits
  • Check for damaged insulation on wires before using them
  • Use one hand when working with live circuits when possible (to avoid creating a path across your heart)
  • Avoid wearing conductive jewelry or accessories while working with electronics

Equipment Usage:

  • Read and understand equipment manuals before operating any device
  • Use equipment only for its intended purpose
  • Verify power settings before connecting to a circuit
  • Turn off equipment when not in use
  • Ensure proper grounding of all equipment
  • Do not defeat safety interlocks or remove protective shields

Soldering Safety: Safety glasses must always be worn when soldering. This requirement applies even if you wear prescription glasses. Wear safety glasses over regular glasses, or use prescription safety glasses. Safety glasses must also be worn when trimming wire leads, as metal pieces can fly upward. If you see someone forget to use safety glasses, remind the person.

Equipment Inspection: Do not use faulty equipment. Inspect the soldering iron power cord for fraying, burns, or exposed wire. Report faulty equipment immediately.

Lead-free Solder: Use lead-free solder whenever possible.

Proper Positioning: Avoid placing your face directly over the work when soldering.

Hair Safety: Tie back long hair so it cannot get burned by the soldering iron or caught in equipment.

Soldering Iron Placement: Always return the soldering iron to its stand. Do not lay it on the workbench.

Power Off: Always turn off the soldering iron and all electronic equipment when you are done using them.

Hygiene: Wash your hands with soap and water when you finish soldering and after handling electronic components.

Equipment Reporting: If bench equipment is not working properly, report it to Lance Halsted (lehalsted@ucdavis.edu) or the teaching assistant.

Emergency Procedures:

  • Know the location of emergency exits, fire extinguishers, and first aid kits
  • In case of electrical fire, never use water; use only appropriate fire extinguishers
  • Know how to shut off main power in case of emergency
  • If an alarm sounds during a lab, immediately vacate Kemper Hall and gather outside the north entrance to Kemper Hall

Component Disposal: Dispose of electronic waste, batteries, and components in the designated containers only.

Conclusion of a Lab

Maintaining a safe and organized laboratory is a collective responsibility. Before leaving, please ensure your workspace is ready for the next person and support your peers in doing the same:

Mutual Reminders: If you notice a fellow student has forgotten to power down their station or clear their area, kindly remind them.

Power Down: Confirm that all bench instruments and electronic equipment are turned off.

Soldering Safety: Verify that the soldering iron is turned off and has been returned to its stand.

Stow Equipment: Return all connecting wires to the designated racks and ensure the bench is free of debris.

Final Reporting: If you encounter a station left in an unsafe state or with faulty equipment, report it to the teaching assistant or lehalsted@ucdavis.edu.

Hygiene: Always wash your hands with soap and water after you finish your work.
