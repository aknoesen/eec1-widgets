  1. Three-area TA scaffolding structure. Each team of three designates one member per area. The role is lead-and-integrator, not sole implementer. All members attend all workshops.
  2. Rishad Joarder: Cloud & Data Layer (ThingSpeak)
  3. Elnaz Hamdarsi: App Designer & Live UI

  4. Basheer Ammar: Sensor Signal Processing & Event Detection

  5. Two lecture slots repurposed for foundation workshops. Full lecture attendance, 100 minutes each, hands-on component required.

  6. Tue May 12, 5:10 to 6:50 PM: Elnaz, Advanced GUI for both tracks (Lecture 13 slot; Data Viz II lecture moved)

  7. Thu May 14, 5:10 to 6:50 PM: Rishad, ThingSpeak for both tracks (Lecture 14 slot; MATLAB Data Structures lecture moved)

  8. No live in-class demonstration. Videos are the primary evaluation artifact. Peer and TA review of videos is the full assessment. All docs cleaned of live-demo references.

  9. Two peer reviews per team (standardized; earlier drafts had inconsistent "two" vs "three" references that are now resolved).

  10. Track rankings captured in the team declaration survey. Each student submits individually listing the other two team members; three matching submissions confirm the team. Students rank Track A vs. Track B; first choice usually granted but second may be assigned for balance.

  11. Video format aligned across Track A, Track B, and Assessment Guide: unlisted YouTube, 6 minutes maximum, six required sections (intro / core functionality demo / reach feature demo / architecture walkthrough / quantitative results summary / one lesson learned), captions required, all three team members on camera or voiceover.

  12. Complementary tasks balance the natural size asymmetry: Elnaz carries three workshops (largest intrinsic area, no additional task); Rishad runs the automated grading harness; Basheer records reference datasets.

  13. Teammate evaluation form added as a required submission item (item 7 in the assessment list). Each student independently rates their teammates on six statements at the end of the project. Confidential, instructor-only, used to identify contribution imbalances that may warrant individual grade adjustments. Statements: contribution to discussions; leadership; respect and consensus; on-time task completion; team focus; would work with again. Distinct from the two peer review forms of other teams' videos.
