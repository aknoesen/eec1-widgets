ENG6 Final Project: TA Scaffolding Plan

Audience: Rishad Joarder, Elnaz Hamdarsi, Basheer Ammar
Project period: Tue May 12 through Thu Jun 4, 2026 (plus Jun 5 to 12 grading window)
Appointments: 25% each (approximately 10 hours/week)

Purpose

The final project spans two tracks (A: Kemper Hall Tracker, B: Networked Sensor Game). Across both tracks, three cross-cutting technical areas drive almost every team's hardest debugging time. Each TA owns one area end to end. Each team of three designates one member as area expert for each of the three areas. The expert is the team's area lead and designated integrator, not the sole implementer; all team members attend all workshops.

This plan explains what each TA owns, how hours are budgeted against the actual course schedule, and how we split grading.

Area 1: Cloud & Data Layer (Rishad Joarder)

Scope

ThingSpeak channel design, field encoding, write patterns, polling reads using the newer-than-last-read-entry pattern, rate-limit-aware decimation, replay, and history queries. Both tracks depend on ThingSpeak; the patterns are the same but the data shapes differ. Track A's typical load is one channel per team with eight fields and roughly one record per 15 seconds. Track B's typical load is a game state channel with compact move strings plus possible secondary channels for chat or leaderboard.

Owns

  • Two workshops (see schedule below)
  • Area-specific consultation hours (time budget below)
  • Reference document "ThingSpeak for ENG6 Final Projects" posted on Canvas by Fri, May 15
  • Complementary task 1: submission intake and Category 1 automated check. Runs Jun 5 (day after submission): ThingSpeak URLs reachable, channels populated with the required minimum records, code folder parses.
  • Complementary task 2: automated grading harness for Category 2. Runs Jun 5 to 11 against each team's ThingSpeak channel and submitted code, using Basheer's reference datasets as inputs.

Area 3: Sensor Signal Processing & Event Detection (Basheer Ammar)

Scope

Threshold tuning, moving-window detection, resultant acceleration, and the sensitivity vs. specificity tradeoff from Mini-Project 3 applied to live streams. For Track A this is stair climb detection, elevator start and stop detection, step counting, and the GPS horizontal-accuracy threshold for regime switching. For Track B this is gesture detection on a moving window with movmean smoothing, plus optional per-player calibration for the calibration reach feature.

Owns

  • Two workshops (see schedule below)
  • Area-specific consultation hours (time budget below)
  • Reference document "Event Detection from Phone Sensors for ENG6 Final Projects" posted on Canvas by Fri, May 15

  • Track B: gesture streams (gesture_stream_1.mat with 12 annotated shakes over 90 seconds; gesture_stream_2.mat with 60 seconds of mixed motion and zero intentional shakes; gesture_truth.json)

Why the lecture-slot placement matters. Workshops 1 and 2 are in full-lecture slots in Week 1, which means 100 minutes of instruction time, every student in the room, and the technical-foundation anchor laid before any team has to make commitments about architecture, channel design, or live-view strategy. Both Elnaz and Rishad should plan for a working hands-on component inside the 100 minutes, not just slides.

Basheer's foundation on Fri May 15. This sits two days after the two lecture-slot workshops, so teams have already seen ThingSpeak and the App Designer structure. Detector architecture builds on MP3/MP4 patterns and does not need to go first.

Week 3 advanced workshops. Both Rishad's and Elnaz's Week 3 workshops (May 27 and May 28) assume teams have a working end-to-end system and are tuning it. Lectures 17 and 18 on those days (5:10 to 6:50 PM) are listed as group-project work sessions; the workshops sit earlier in the afternoon, not in conflict.

No Basheer workshop in Week 3. Basheer's Week 3 hours shift to reference dataset annotation and the harness dry-run with Rishad (see time budget).

Consultation Hours

Two categories:
1. Lab section coverage. Each TA is assigned a subset of the 8 lab sections during Module 7 (May 12 to 18) and Module 9 (May 26 to Jun 1). This is general consultation: whichever team walks in, whatever question they have. Each TA likely covers 2 to 3 sections per week.
2. Area-specific office hours. Each TA additionally holds 1 to 2 hours per week of dedicated area consultation. Teams post questions on the course forum first; area-tagged questions route to the corresponding TA's slot.

Bring your preferred office-hours slots to today's meeting and we will finalize.
