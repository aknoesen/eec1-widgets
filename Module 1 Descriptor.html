Module 1: Foundations and Circuit Language

Module 1 is your entry point into electrical engineering. You will build a conceptual foundation for how charge, current, and voltage work, learn to read and draw circuit schematics, and get hands-on with the ADALM2000 (M2K) for the first time.

This module covers:
* Electric force, field, and potential using a gravity-based analogy
* Conventional current and the difference between DC and AC
* Circuit schematics: nodes, branches, loops, and ground conventions
* Responsible AI use as a learning tool in engineering

The Physics Behind the Circuit

We begin with the "why" before the "what." Using a gravity and hill analogy, you will learn to distinguish three foundational concepts: electric force, electric field, and electric potential. These invisible quantities are the reason a circuit does anything at all. Understanding the difference between them is the first step toward reading and building real circuits.

Speaking the Language of Circuits

Engineers communicate circuit ideas through schematics, not physical pictures of wires and components. You will learn to read and draw schematics using standard symbols and master the vocabulary of nodes, branches, and loops. You will also learn the conventions for current direction, voltage polarity, and ground references that apply throughout the rest of the course.

Responsible AI Use

This course treats AI as a thinking partner, not an answer dispenser. In Module 1, you will be introduced to how AI tools should and should not be used in your engineering education. Critical evaluation, proper attribution, and verification against the textbook are professional habits you will practice from the start.

Post-Lecture Assessments

After completing the lecture and lab, you will take a post-lecture assessment for each chapter. These questions go beyond recall . You will need to apply concepts, predict outcomes, and explain reasoning. Questions are drawn at random from a bank; each quiz is 6 questions · 15 minutes.

How to prepare: For each topic below, ask your AI tool for a worked example or scenario, then solve it yourself. If you can explain your reasoning out loud, you are ready.

Chapter 1: Electrical Foundations

  • Electric charge and force — how charges attract and repel; how distance and magnitude affect the force
  • Electric field and potential — what a field represents; what potential difference means physically; the gravity/hill analogy
  • The force–field–potential hierarchy — how force, field, and potential relate to each other and to their gravitational counterparts
  • Current — the direction convention for conventional current; why it is opposite to electron flow; DC vs AC
  • A complete circuit — the four functional parts; how energy is converted from source to load

Chapter 2: Circuit Language

  • Schematics — what a schematic captures and what it deliberately omits
  • Standard symbols — resistor, voltage source, switch, wire, ground
  • Circuit topology — nodes, branches, and loops; identifying each in a schematic
  • Connection conventions — connected junction vs crossing wires; why all points on an ideal wire share the same potential
  • Open and short circuits — the effect of each on current flow in the surrounding circuit
  • Voltage and current conventions — applying correct polarity signs; marking conventional current direction given a source
