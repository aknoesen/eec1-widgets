Module 2 introduces the analytical tools you will use throughout your engineering career. You will move from understanding individual components to analyzing how they behave in connected networks, and you will verify those laws in the lab.

Conservation Laws in Action

KCL and KVL are not just textbook rules. They are statements of conservation of charge and energy. Every method of circuit analysis you will encounter in this course derives from these two laws. You will apply them to resistor networks and verify that they hold in the real world on your breadboard.

The Voltage Divider

If there is one circuit to master in Module 2, it is the voltage divider. This two-resistor configuration appears in almost every engineering application. You will learn how it works in theory and how its output voltage changes when a load is connected, an effect called loading. This loading concept will resurface when you study op-amps in Module 6.

The I-V Menagerie

Every component can be characterized by its I-V curve, a graph that shows how current through it relates to voltage across it. Resistors produce a straight line; diodes do not. You will be introduced to this range of component behaviors as a preview of the device characterization you will carry further in Module 3.
