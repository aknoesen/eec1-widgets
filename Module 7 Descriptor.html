Measuring Small Signals in a Noisy World

Real sensor signals are often tiny and ride on a large common-mode voltage that appears equally on both signal leads. A simple difference amplifier can cancel this background in principle, but its finite input impedance distorts the sensor signal. The three-op-amp instrumentation amplifier solves this by presenting nearly infinite input impedance while rejecting common-mode interference with high precision.

The INA125 Instrumentation Amplifier

The INA125 is a complete instrumentation amplifier in a single package, with gain set by one external resistor. For the scale project, the Wheatstone bridge load cell produces only about 10 mV at full load, riding on a 2.5 V common-mode voltage. The INA125 amplifies the differential signal by approximately 400 times while rejecting the common-mode background, leaving a clean signal ready for the next stage.

Signal Chain Design

Amplification alone is not enough. To get the best resolution from the ADC, the conditioned signal must fill the full ADC input range. The summing amplifier acts as a level shifter, mapping the amplifier output to the ADC's input window. Every design decision in the chain, from gain to offset to signal polarity, exists to maximize the fraction of the ADC range occupied by the signal of interest.
